Large Tree Removal Questions — Answered Honestly

Straightforward answers to the questions Huntsville homeowners ask us most.

How much does large tree removal cost in Huntsville, AL?

Large tree removal in Huntsville typically runs:

  • 40–60 ft: $800 – $1,500
  • 60–80 ft: $1,500 – $3,000
  • 80 ft and taller: $3,000 – $5,000+
  • Over a house / crane-required: $3,500 – $6,000+

Price is driven by height, trunk diameter, lean, how close the tree is to structures, and whether a crane is required. See our full Huntsville tree removal cost guide.

Can you take down a tree over my house or in a tight backyard?
Yes — that's our specialty. Our remote-controlled tree-removal truck fits through most gates and reaches spots a bucket truck can't. We rig large trees down piece by piece so nothing hits the structure.
Do I need a permit to remove a tree?
Most private-property removals in Huntsville don't require a permit. Trees in public rights-of-way, historic districts, or HOA-governed areas may. We always confirm local rules before starting.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes — Alabama Tree Surgery (TS) license, general liability insurance specific to tree work (not generic landscaping), and workers' compensation on every employee. Certificates available on request.
Do you offer 24/7 emergency service?
Yes. Call (256) 497-8735 any time — we dispatch emergency crews around the clock for fallen trees, storm damage, and hazards threatening structures or power lines.
How long does a typical tree removal take?
Most residential removals wrap up in 2–6 hours, including cleanup. Large hardwoods near homes or crane removals may take a full day. You'll always get a clear time estimate with your quote.
Do you remove the stump?
Stump grinding is offered as a separate service that can be added to any removal. We grind 6–12 inches below grade and can either haul the grindings or leave them to settle in place.
Will homeowners insurance cover the removal?
If a tree damages a covered structure (home, car, fence), insurance typically pays for removal from the damaged structure. Preventive removal of a healthy tree is usually not covered. We document the damage with photos for your claim.
Who's responsible when a neighbor's tree falls on my property?
Alabama generally puts responsibility on the property where the tree falls, not where it grew — unless you can prove the owner knew the tree was hazardous and ignored written notice. Your insurance typically handles your property; theirs handles theirs. See our full guide.
How close to my house can you remove a tree?
With the right equipment — cranes, rigging, and climbing crews — we safely remove trees within inches of homes, pools, and power lines. Complex jobs always get a written plan before any cuts.
Do you take the wood?
Yes, by default we haul away all debris. If you'd like to keep logs for firewood or milling, just let us know and we'll cut them to length and stack them.
How soon can you come out for an estimate?
Usually within 1–3 business days for standard estimates, same-day for emergencies. Call or submit our contact form.
