How Much Does Tree Removal Cost in Huntsville, AL?

Real price ranges from a local certified arborist — plus what drives cost up or down, and how to compare quotes without getting fooled.

The Short Answer

In Huntsville, most residential tree removals run $300 to $2,000. Very large trees near structures or requiring a crane can push $3,500+. Very small, easy-access trees can be under $300.

Cost by Tree Size

$300–$600Small (<30 ft)
$600–$1,200Medium (30–60 ft)
$1,200–$2,000Large (60–80 ft)
$2,000–$3,500+Very large / crane

What Actually Drives the Price

1. Height and trunk diameter

Doubling the height of a tree more than doubles the work. A 60-ft oak isn't twice the job of a 30-ft oak — it's closer to four times.

2. Proximity to structures

A tree standing alone in an open yard can be dropped in one piece. A tree over your house has to be dismantled section by section with ropes, bucket, or crane. That changes the price dramatically.

3. Species and wood weight

Hardwoods like red oak, hickory, and pecan weigh 50–70 lb/cubic foot — nearly twice the density of softwoods like pine. Heavy wood means more rigging, more trips to the truck, and more disposal cost.

4. Access

Can we drive a bucket truck up to the tree? That's the cheapest scenario. Do we have to carry everything through a 36-inch gate into a backyard? That can double the labor time.

5. Whether a crane is required

Crane rentals run $400–$1,200/day on top of crew costs, but they turn a two-day climbing job into a half-day safe removal. For trees near homes, cranes often save money overall by avoiding damage.

6. Stump removal

Grinding the stump is a separate service that adds $100–$350 typically. See our stump grinding page.

7. Debris handling

Hauling all the wood and chips away costs more than leaving mulch on site. Some customers want the firewood — we'll cut it to length at no extra charge.

Red Flags in Cheap Quotes

  • Cash-only, no written estimate. If something goes wrong, you have no recourse.
  • No proof of insurance. If an uninsured tree "guy" damages your house, you're on the hook.
  • "Topping" offered as a shortcut to removal. Topping ruins trees and often leads to more expensive work later.
  • Verbal quote over the phone without seeing the tree. Real estimates happen on site.

How to Compare Apples to Apples

Ask every bidder: Are you insured? Will the quote be in writing? Does it include stump grinding? Does it include debris haul-off? What's your timeline? The lowest number isn't always the cheapest job.

Will Insurance Cover It?

If the tree already damaged a covered structure (house, garage, fence, car), insurance typically pays for removal. Preventive removal of a standing healthy tree is generally not covered. See our FAQ page for more insurance details.
