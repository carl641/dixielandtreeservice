Hazardous Tree Assessment in Huntsville, AL

Written tree risk evaluations by ISA Certified Arborists — for homeowners, HOAs, property managers, insurance claims, and neighbor disputes.

What Is a Tree Risk Assessment?

A formal tree risk assessment uses ISA's Tree Risk Assessment Qualification (TRAQ) framework to evaluate three factors: likelihood of failure, likelihood of impact, and consequences of that impact. The result is a Low/Moderate/High/Extreme rating, with recommendations. Unlike a casual opinion, a written TRAQ assessment is defensible in insurance claims and legal disputes.

When You Need One

  • Insurance claim — proving a tree was or wasn't hazardous before failure
  • Neighbor dispute — written notice of a hazardous tree on adjacent property
  • HOA / property management — documenting tree conditions on common property
  • Pre-purchase inspection — evaluating trees before buying a home
  • Permit support — justifying removal of protected trees
  • Post-storm evaluation — identifying trees destabilized by recent storms

What's in a Written Report

  • Site map with numbered tree locations
  • Species, diameter, and approximate height of each tree
  • Observed defects (cracks, decay, cavities, lean, root damage)
  • Failure and impact likelihood, with rationale
  • Risk rating per tree
  • Prioritized recommendations (remove, mitigate, monitor)
  • Signed by an ISA Certified Arborist

Independent Assessments Available

We offer assessments as a standalone service — no obligation to hire us for any work that results. Independence is what makes a report credible.

Pricing

Single-tree written evaluations typically start at $250. Multi-tree property assessments are quoted by tree count and site complexity. Report delivery is typically within 5 business days.
