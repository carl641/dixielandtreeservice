Storm Damage Tree Cleanup in Huntsville, AL

North Alabama's tornado season, straight-line winds, and ice storms put trees down fast. Our crews restore order — safely and with insurance-ready documentation.

North Alabama Storms Are Different

Huntsville sits in Dixie Alley. From the March–May severe weather season to the summer derecho risk and occasional winter ice events, our trees take a beating. Pines snap, oak leaders split, and shallow-rooted species uproot in saturated soil. Our storm crews are trained for exactly this work.

What We Handle

  • Trees on homes, garages, and outbuildings
  • Fallen trees blocking driveways and roads
  • Hanging limbs (widowmakers) lodged in canopies
  • Uprooted trees leaning on fences or neighbors' structures
  • Split trunks and cracked leaders that may still fail
  • Power line entanglements (coordinated with the utility)

Insurance-Ready Every Time

Storm work is usually an insurance-covered event when a tree damages a structure. We make your claim painless:

  • Detailed photos of damage and tree condition
  • Written arborist report (cause, risk, and recommended work)
  • Itemized invoice with categories adjusters expect
  • Direct communication with your adjuster on request

Emergency Tarping Available

If a tree opened up your roof, we can tarp and stabilize while insurance is sorted. We work with your adjuster on staging.

Common Storm-Prone Species in Huntsville

Loblolly and shortleaf pines dominate our windthrow calls — their shallow root plates fail in saturated soil. Water oaks and red oaks often drop large limbs in wind. Bradford pears split almost on schedule in strong storms. We can discuss preventive pruning or removal of at-risk trees on your property once the immediate storm cleanup is done.

Serving North Alabama 24/7

Storm crews respond across Huntsville, Madison, Harvest, Hazel Green, Athens, Decatur, and surrounding communities.
