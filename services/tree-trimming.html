Tree Trimming & Pruning in Huntsville, AL

Professional pruning to strengthen structure, improve health, and protect your home. ANSI A300-standard cuts from ISA-certified arborists.

Pruning Is Not Just "Cutting Branches"

Bad pruning — especially topping — can ruin a tree permanently. We follow the ANSI A300 pruning standard used by municipalities and utilities across the country. That means every cut is made at the branch collar, weight distribution is preserved, and no more than 25% of live crown is removed in a single pruning.

Types of Pruning We Perform

  • Crown cleaning — removing dead, diseased, and broken branches
  • Crown thinning — selective removal of live branches to reduce density and wind load
  • Crown reduction — reducing overall size while preserving tree structure
  • Clearance pruning — clearing roofs, gutters, driveways, power lines, and pools
  • Structural pruning — training young trees for strong long-term form
  • Deadwooding — removing dead limbs before they fall on their own

When to Prune Trees in North Alabama

For most hardwoods — hickory, sweetgum, pecan, maple — late winter (January through early March) is ideal. The tree is dormant, disease pressure is low, and the fresh scaffold can be seen clearly.

Oaks are different. In Alabama, we avoid pruning oaks April through July to prevent oak wilt transmission by sap-feeding beetles. Dead limbs can be removed any time; live pruning waits for cooler months.

We Never Top Trees

"Topping" — cutting back a tree to stubs — causes decay, weak regrowth, and eventual failure. If you want a tree smaller, ask us about proper crown reduction.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I prune my trees?
Mature hardwoods generally need pruning every 3–5 years. Young trees benefit from light structural pruning annually.
Will pruning hurt my tree?
Done correctly, no — it improves health. Done wrong (topping, flush cuts), it causes permanent damage.
Can you prune around power lines?
Yes, for utility-adjacent clearance. For trees touching primary lines, the utility may need to de-energize first.
