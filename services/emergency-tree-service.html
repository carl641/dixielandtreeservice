When to Call for Emergency Tree Service

  • Tree fell on your home, garage, shed, fence, or vehicle
  • Tree down across your driveway or blocking access
  • Branch or tree hanging into or over your house
  • Tree leaning dramatically after a storm
  • Tree contacting or threatening power lines
  • Large cracked limb hanging in the canopy ("widowmaker")

If Power Lines Are Involved

Never approach a tree touching a live power line. Call the utility (Huntsville Utilities, Athens Utilities, TVA, or your provider) to de-energize before we remove the tree. We coordinate with all North Alabama utilities routinely.

What to Do Before the Crew Arrives

  1. Keep everyone away from the tree — including pets. Cracked wood can shift without warning.
  2. Don't move anything major. Leave debris where it is for insurance photos.
  3. Take your own photos of the damage and any items crushed or threatened.
  4. Call your homeowners insurance to open a claim — we can send our documentation directly to your adjuster.
  5. Shut off gas at the meter if a tree struck the home hard.

Emergency Tree Service Pricing

Emergency response carries a premium over scheduled work because crews are being pulled from other jobs, night/weekend work is involved, and equipment stays on the job longer. Expect pricing typically 25–50% higher than a standard scheduled removal. We provide clear pricing up front before any work begins.

Insurance & Emergency Tree Work

In most cases, a tree that has damaged a covered structure is an insurance-covered event. We provide:

  • Detailed photos and written reports for your claim
  • Itemized invoices insurers expect
  • Direct coordination with your adjuster if you approve
  • Emergency stabilization (tarping, propping) before full removal
