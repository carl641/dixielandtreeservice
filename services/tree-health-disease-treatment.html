Tree Health & Disease Treatment in Huntsville, AL

Certified arborist diagnosis and treatment for the diseases, pests, and decline syndromes common to North Alabama trees.

Diagnosis First, Treatment Second

Too many "tree doctors" recommend treatments before identifying the problem. We start with an ISA-certified arborist evaluation: species, site conditions, canopy health, root flare, pest signs, and a written diagnosis. Only then do we discuss whether treatment, pruning, or removal is the right answer.

Common Tree Problems in North Alabama

  • Oak wilt — fatal fungal disease spread by beetles; prevention via seasonal pruning
  • Bacterial leaf scorch — red and pin oak decline, worse in drought years
  • Anthracnose — spring leaf disease affecting sycamore, dogwood, and oak
  • Pine bark beetles — southern pine beetle and Ips engraver beetles
  • Emerald ash borer — now confirmed in North Alabama; kills untreated ash
  • Fusiform rust — galls on loblolly and slash pine
  • Hypoxylon canker — opportunistic fungus on drought-stressed oak
  • Root rot (armillaria, phytophthora) — soil-borne decline
  • Girdling roots — often the hidden cause of "sudden" decline
  • Construction damage — root compaction and grade changes

Treatments We Offer

  • Deep root fertilization — targeted feeding where tree roots actually live
  • Trunk injection — for emerald ash borer, oak wilt, and other systemic needs
  • Preventive sprays — timed for specific pest life cycles
  • Soil decompaction & aeration — for trees suffering construction damage
  • Pruning for disease — selective removal of infected wood with sanitized tools
  • Cabling & bracing — see our cabling service

When to Call for a Health Check

Early, thinning canopy; yellowing between veins; premature leaf drop; mushrooms at the base; fine sawdust on bark; or sudden decline of one side. Any of these warrants a professional look.

Our Pledge: Honest Recommendations

Some trees can be saved. Some can't. We'll tell you which is which — and we don't upsell treatments to trees that would be better replaced.
