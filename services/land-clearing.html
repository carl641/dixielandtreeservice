Land & Lot Clearing in Huntsville, AL

Partial or full clearing for homesites, additions, fence lines, pastures, and builder lots. Efficient equipment, careful tree preservation where you want it, and thorough cleanup.

Clearing for Any Project

  • New homesite preparation — clearing building pads, driveways, septic fields, and yard
  • Builder & developer lots — production-speed clearing for neighborhood projects
  • Fence line clearing — narrow corridors for new fence runs or property lines
  • Pasture reclamation — cleaning up overgrown pasture and cedar encroachment
  • Selective clearing — preserving specimen hardwoods while removing underbrush
  • Right-of-way & driveway clearing — creating access to interior lots

How We Clear

Our approach depends on the project. For a selective lot where you want to keep mature oaks, we work tree by tree with climbers and bucket trucks. For production clearing on a builder lot, we bring in mini-skids, mulchers, and chippers to move quickly. Either way, we protect what stays, map out save-zones, and leave the site ready for the next trade.

Burn, Haul, or Mulch?

Debris handling is a major cost driver. Options:

  • Chip and spread — chips left on site as mulch or erosion control
  • Haul off — complete removal of all debris
  • Burn on site — where permitted by county burn rules
  • Grind in place — forestry mulcher creates a finished surface

Permit & Erosion Control

Large clearings may require NPDES/erosion-control permits in Alabama. We coordinate with your builder or engineer on silt-fence installation and compliance.

Cost & Timeline

Land clearing is typically priced per acre and varies widely based on tree density, slope, and debris handling. Light clearing can run $1,500–$3,000/acre; heavy timber with haul-off can exceed $8,000/acre. We provide fixed-price proposals after an on-site walk.

Serving All of North Alabama

We clear lots throughout Madison, Limestone, and Morgan counties. High demand in Harvest, Monrovia, Toney, Hazel Green, and New Market where new neighborhoods are going in fast.
