Tree Service in Madison, AL

Professional tree care from the Bridge Street area to the Limestone County line. Fast response for Madison homeowners, builders, and HOAs.

Tree Care for Madison's Growing Neighborhoods

Madison's explosive growth over the past 20 years planted a lot of suburban-mix trees that are now reaching problem size. Subdivisions from the late 90s and early 2000s planted heavily in Bradford pear — and those trees split on a schedule. Newer builds lean on river birch, red maple, and crepe myrtle. Meanwhile the older Madison Boulevard and Sullivan Street corridors still have mature sweetgum, water oak, and loblolly pine that need experienced care.

Services We Provide in Madison

  • Tree removal — including split Bradford pears and dying pines
  • Pruning & crown reduction for maturing maples and birches
  • Stump grinding — same-day service in most Madison subdivisions
  • 24/7 emergency response — fast dispatch from nearby crews
  • Lot clearing for new construction west of Madison Boulevard

Common Issues We See in Madison

  • Bradford pear splits — especially during spring storms
  • Pine beetle decline in older loblolly stands
  • Sudden limb drop on mature water oaks
  • Root heave where trees are planted too close to foundations
  • Fast-growing river birches reaching sizes beyond their planting spot

Madison Permit Notes

City of Madison generally does not require a permit for private-property removals. Some HOA-governed neighborhoods (certain Clift's Cove, Stonegate, and Heritage Plantation sections) have additional rules. We check before we start.
