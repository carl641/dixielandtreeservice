Tree Service in Meridianville, AL

Rural-suburban tree care for northern Madison County, from U.S. 231 north to the Tennessee line.

Meridianville Tree Work

Meridianville's larger residential lots and ag-adjacent properties mean we see a wide range of work here: single trees over homes, fence-line clearing, hazardous pecan removals on older homesteads, and storm response after the regular squall lines that cross north Madison County. The area's mix of open pasture and hardwood groves means wind exposure is high — so is windthrow risk.

What We Handle in Meridianville

  • Large-lot tree removals with bucket truck or crane access
  • Pecan pruning — heavy nut load stresses weak branch unions
  • Post oak and eastern red cedar management
  • Storm damage — rural crews often wait longer for response; we don't let you
  • Fence-line and pasture-edge clearing

Common Species

Post oak, southern red oak, pecan, shortleaf pine, eastern red cedar, and hackberry all show up routinely here. Ice-storm damage to pecan is a regular winter call. In summer, drought-stressed oaks are the main concern.

Areas We Serve

Meridianville 35759, Hazel Green border, Moores Mill area, Winchester Road corridor, and north to the Tennessee line.
