Tree Service in Athens, AL

Certified arborist tree care throughout Athens and Limestone County — from Athens State University's historic campus to the rural farmsteads of north Limestone.

Athens Has Some of North Alabama's Oldest Trees

The historic district around Athens State University holds white oaks, post oaks, and southern magnolias that predate the Civil War. These trees need careful pruning (no topping, ever), occasional cabling, and arborist-led risk management — not a chainsaw crew. Dixie Land has the TRAQ-qualified staff to assess and preserve them.

Outside town, Limestone County's mix of row-crop agriculture and recent residential subdivisions means a lot of land clearing, fence-line work, and pasture reclaim alongside residential removals.

Athens Services

  • Tree removal
  • Structural pruning of mature historic-district trees
  • Cabling & bracing for old specimens worth preserving
  • Stump grinding
  • Land & pasture clearing across Limestone County
  • 24/7 emergency response

Common Athens Tree Issues

  • Aging white oaks in the historic district
  • Southern magnolia storm damage — heavy wood breaks dramatically
  • Eastern red cedar encroachment in unmanaged pastureland
  • Storm-damaged pecan — especially in older rural homesteads
  • Hackberry decline in urban plantings

Areas We Serve

Athens, Ardmore, Elkmont, Tanner, Mooresville, Rogersville, all of 35611, 35613, 35614, and the rest of Limestone County.
