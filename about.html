The Remote Controlled Tree Service

Family-owned by Robert Norman, Dixieland Tree Service has taken care of trees across Huntsville, Madison County and Limestone County for more than 30 years — where old-school values and new technology meet.

Our Story

Robert Norman started Dixieland Tree Service more than three decades ago with a simple promise: treat every customer's property like it was his own. Thirty-plus years and thousands of jobs later, that standard hasn't changed. We still do the work safely, do it right, and leave the property better than we found it — and we've earned an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau of North Alabama and a Google Guaranteed badge along the way.

Why "Remote Controlled"?

A lot of tree companies show up with heavy trucks and drive them straight across your yard. We don't. Dixieland operates a remote-controlled tree-removal truck that can reach into tight spaces, cut from the street, and keep heavy equipment off your lawn, driveway, and landscaping. It's safer for our crew, safer for your property, and it's the reason neighbors tell us, "You'd never know they were here."

Licensed, Insured, BBB A+ Accredited

Credentials You Can Verify

BBB A+ Accredited (North Alabama) · Google Guaranteed · Alabama Tree Surgery (TS) License · General Liability Insurance · Workers' Compensation (Alabama). Certificates available on request.

Alabama requires tree services to carry a Tree Surgery (TS) Commercial Applicator Permit through the Department of Agriculture, plus a Horticulture Professional Services License. We carry both. We also carry general liability insurance specific to tree work — not generic landscaper coverage — and workers' compensation on every employee.

Built for North Alabama

We grew up around these trees. Huntsville, Madison County, and Limestone County have their own particular challenges: fast-moving storm systems from the west, tornado seasons that leave pines down across neighborhoods, and a mix of hardwoods — oak, hickory, pecan, sweetgum — that each demand their own approach. After 30+ years working these neighborhoods, our crews know the species, the soil, and the weather that shapes them.

Our Equipment & Process

  • Remote-controlled tree-removal truck for tight access and minimal yard impact
  • Bucket trucks for high-reach removals and pruning over structures
  • Climbing crews for specimen trees and technical rigging
  • Stump grinders that reach into backyards through standard gates
  • Mini-skids and chippers for efficient cleanup
  • Cranes available for oversized removals near homes

What You Can Expect

  1. Honest assessment — If a tree doesn't need to come down, we'll say so.
  2. Clear written estimate — Scope, price, and timeline on one page.
  3. Safe, tidy work — Ropes, mats, and plywood protect what stays.
  4. Complete cleanup — Chipped brush, raked leaves, blown-off hardscapes.
